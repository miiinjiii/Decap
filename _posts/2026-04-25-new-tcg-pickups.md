Recently, I picked up some new Magic: The Gathering products that I was really excited about.

I bought the Commander decks *Silverquill Statement* and *Witherbloom Witchcraft* from the Strixhaven series, and I also started building a new Standard Life Gain deck.

Both gave me new ideas for deckbuilding and helped me discover which playstyles I enjoy the most.

![](/images/uploads/9151854_1.jpg)

- - -

## Silverquill Influence

Silverquill is based on white and black mana and focuses on politics, counters, and strong board interaction.

What I like about this deck is that it feels very flexible.

You can play aggressively, support temporary alliances in multiplayer games, or slowly build value over time.

I especially enjoy how the deck rewards good timing and smart decisions instead of only raw power.

It feels very interactive and fits well for Commander games with multiple players.

- - -

## Witherbloom Pestilence

Witherbloom is based on black and green mana and focuses on life gain, sacrificing resources, and creating value from small advantages.

This deck became one of my favorites very quickly.

The constant balance between gaining life, sacrificing creatures, and building long-term advantage feels very satisfying.

It taught me that slower value engines can often be stronger than fast explosive plays.

Because of this deck, I also discovered that I really enjoy Golgari playstyles.

- - -

## My New Interest in Golgari

Since playing Witherbloom, I found a strong affinity for Golgari (Black/Green).

I really like the combination of:

* graveyard value
* sacrifice mechanics
* life gain and drain
* strong recursion
* long-term resource advantage

Golgari decks feel very strategic and rewarding because every small decision matters.

Instead of trying to win quickly, I prefer building value step by step and controlling the game over time.

That playstyle fits me much better.

- - -

## Standard Life Gain Deck

Besides Commander, I also wanted to build something for Standard.

I decided to go with a Life Gain strategy because it connects well with the kind of gameplay I already enjoy in Golgari and Witherbloom decks.

The deck focuses on:

* gaining life consistently
* strong creature synergy
* board control
* long-term value

Life Gain decks may look simple at first, but good sequencing and resource management make them very rewarding to play.

- - -

## Why I Enjoy Both

Commander gives me creativity and longer multiplayer games.

Standard gives me cleaner decisions and stronger one-on-one matches.

Playing both formats keeps the game fresh and helps me improve as a player.

That is one of the reasons why Magic: The Gathering remains one of my favorite hobbies.

Both are very different, but that is exactly what makes TCGs so interesting for me.

The new Secret of Strixhaven decks immediately caught my attention because of their strong themes, interesting mechanics, and the overall design.
